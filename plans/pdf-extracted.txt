

===== PAGE 1 =====

An effective solution is a multi-layered AI system that ingests global data streams, normalizes
them into a common ontology, and maintains a continuously updating knowledge graph that
can be queried for strategic, explainable insights in real time. Below is a practical,
implementation-oriented blueprint you can directly adapt into a hackathon or SIH proposal.
The engine should act as a “semantic operating system” for world events: it continuously
absorbs data from geopolitics, economics, defense, technology, climate, and society, converts it
into structured entities and relations, and exposes an intelligence graph for interactive
analysis. The primary goals are:
Unify structured, unstructured, and streaming data into a single ontology-backed
knowledge graph.
Enable powerful, multi-hop strategic questions (e.g., “How do recent semiconductor export
controls affect India’s defense readiness and trade?”).
Provide transparent, auditable reasoning paths (data lineage, sources, timestamps) for
national and global decision ‑ makers.
Design and develop an AI-powered Global
Ontology Engine that can collect and understand
structured data, unstructured content, and live
real-tim e feeds from  areas like geopolitics,
econom ics, defense, technology, clim ate, and
society— and then connect all of it into a single,
unified, constantly updating intelligence graph,
so decision-m akers can get clear insights for
strategy, transparency, and national advantage
for India and the world.

You can think of the system in four main layers, similar to modern graph-powered intelligence
platforms and ontology-driven AI stacks.
Layer Role Key Technologies (examples)
Data Ingestion Collect and normalize multi-format data Kafka/Flink, Airbyte, custom ETL
Understanding & Enrichment NLP, entity linking, normalization LLMs, NER/NEL, classifiers
Ontology & Graph Store Canonical schema and relationships Neo4j / GraphDB, RDF/OW L, SHACL
Intelligence & Delivery Querying, analytics, UI, APIs GraphRAG, dashboards, alerting
This mirrors how tools like Palantir Foundry and GraphAware Hume integrate heterogeneous
feeds into an always-updated intelligence graph for national security and operations.
You want a mix of:
Structured: World Bank indicators, IMF data, trade statistics, military expenditure
databases, election results, climate indices.
Unstructured: News, research reports, think ‑ tank analyses, policy documents, defense
white papers, social media (filtered OSINT).
Real-time/stream: Newswires, RSS, selected social feeds, market data, satellite-derived
alerts (e.g., climate anomalies).
Use a streaming backbone (Kafka/Flink) to handle live feeds and batch connectors
(Airbyte/custom ETL) to pull databases on schedule.
Normalize everything into a common event envelope (source, time, geo, modality, domain
tag), inspired by real-time fusion platforms in security and national-intel use cases.
In GraphRAG-style systems, the ontology is the blueprint that defines what entities and
relations exist and how they connect. For this PS, your top-level classes might include:
Actors: Country, Organization, Company, Individual, Armed Group.
Systems: WeaponSystem, Technology, Infrastructure, Platform.

You can follow a “top ‑ down + LLM ‑ assisted” approach: define base ontology manually, then
refine/extend using LLM tools as shown in ontology-driven KG examples with RDF + Neo4j.
Use a labeled property graph (Neo4j) or RDF triple store with SHACL constraints to ensure
data consistency and allow reasoning.
Apply ontology reasoners to infer new facts (e.g., if Company X is sanctioned and supplies
Component Y to Sector Z in India, infer potential supply ‑ chain risk in that sector).
From unstructured text, you need:
Named Entity Recognition and Linking (countries, leaders, companies, treaties) tied to
canonical IDs.
Relation extraction (e.g., “Country A imposed sanctions on Company B in Sector C”).
Event extraction with type + time + location + involved actors.
Recent work shows pipelines that use NER + linking over large news streams to build
geopolitical knowledge graphs for situational awareness and national security leaders.
Domain classifiers: tag documents into geopolitics, economics, defense, tech, climate,
society, or multi-label combos.
Risk/impact models: estimate severity scores (economic risk, conflict escalation, climate
vulnerability) using ML on historical patterns.
Implement a GraphRAG pipeline: semantic search for relevant text, traverse the knowledge
graph neighborhood, then feed both into an LLM for an answer.
This improves accuracy and multi-hop reasoning vs. plain RAG because the ontology tells
the system which links matter.

===== PAGE 6 =====

Data quality and misinformation: “garbage in, garbage out” is a known challenge in OSINT
and news-derived graphs; include source scoring, credibility labels, and human review
loops.
Privacy and compliance: respect data protection (e.g., DPDP Act–style constraints), store
only necessary PII, and log access to sensitive entities.
Over-automation: keep humans in the loop— tools like Hume and Palantir emphasize
analyst ‑ centric workflows rather than fully automated decisions.
Data/ML: NLP, entity linking, basic graph algorithms, LLM integration.
Knowledge Engineering: ontology modeling, RDF/OW L, SHACL, Neo4j or RDF store.
Backend/Streaming: APIs, Kafka/Flink, ETL pipelines.
Frontend/UX: graph visualization, dashboards for non-technical decision ‑ makers.
Domain Experts: geopolitics/econ/defense/climate for ontology design and validation.
 . Scope & Ontology Draft
Pick priority domains and define the first ontology version (entities/relations).
 . Data POC
Ingest small curated datasets + limited news corpus; run extraction pipeline to
populate a minimal KG.
 . GraphRAG Prototype
W ire KG to a GraphRAG QA service for a few complex queries.
 . Visualization & Stories
Build dashboards and 2–3 “policy stories” (e.g., “How AI export controls intersect with
Indian semiconductor policy and global alliances”).
 . Refine & Harden
Improve entity resolution, add more sources, and tune ontology based on feedback.
This blueprint gives you a concrete, credible path from high-level vision to an implementable
architecture, while clearly differentiating your “Global Ontology Engine” from existing
knowledge-graph and intelligence platforms.
